A long line of volcanoes () extends from the Pacific Ocean () on eastward to the Gulf of Mexico, just to the south of Veracruz. The very narrow coastal plain along the Pacific Ocean coastline rise quickly into the foothills of the mountains, while the Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean coastlines are wider and rise gently into the interior. Mexico's limestone Yucatan Peninsula is tree-covered, with thick tropical jungles along it borders with Central America countries.
