Cass County (Missouri, USA)
Cass County, Missouri U.S. Census TIGER/Line map
Cass County, , approximately 40 miles (65 km) southeast of , in the mid-1950s contained a Mennonite congregation ([Old] Mennonite Church, USA) of 250 members. For many years it had two congregations, numbering in 1908 a total of 480 members. Amish Mennonite settlers moved into the area as early as 1860, but regular church services were not held until 1866. Two years later they organized the . Out of this church developed the Sycamore Grove and the congregations, both in the area in the southeastern part of the county.
